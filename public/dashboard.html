KPI Definitions
Exact formulas for every metric in the system — no ambiguity
Revenue/hour
SUM(trip.fare + trip.tips) / SUM(shift.active_hours)
Active hours = app ON and available. Excludes breaks and app-off time. Calculated per driver per shift. Fleet average = weighted mean. Numerator uses gross fare before commission deduction.
Trips/hour
COUNT(trips WHERE status='completed') / SUM(shift.active_hours)
Completed trips only. Cancelled and no-shows excluded from numerator but shift hours count in denominator — this correctly penalizes cancellation patterns.
Utilization rate
SUM(busy_minutes) / (SUM(busy_minutes) + SUM(idle_minutes)) × 100
Busy = en-route to pickup + on active trip. Idle = app ON, no trip. Break time and app-off time excluded from both. Pure productive vs waiting ratio.
Idle time%
SUM(idle_minutes) / SUM(total_shift_minutes) × 100
Denominator includes break time — more conservative than utilization. Used for scheduling optimization. Differs from utilization rate by including non-app time in denominator.
Profit/vehicle
Revenue − (driver_payout + fuel + daily_lease + daily_insurance + amortized_maintenance + daily_depreciation)
Per vehicle per day. Maintenance amortized = monthly_cost / 30. Depreciation = (purchase − residual) / useful_life_days. Must be calculated individually — fleet average masks individual losses.
Driver score
(Rev/hr_norm×0.30) + (Util×0.25) + (Accept×0.20) + (Trips/hr_norm×0.15) + (Cancel_score×0.10) Cancel_score = MAX(0, 100 − cancel_rate% × 5) All components capped at 100
Recalculated daily at midnight. Tier classification uses 7-day rolling average. Weights reflect business priority: revenue efficiency first, time efficiency second.
Acceptance rate
COUNT(accepted_trips) / COUNT(total_trip_offers) × 100
Source from platform API directly — Bolt and Uber maintain rolling windows (typically last 100 offers). Mirror platform calculation to align with what platforms report and base deactivation decisions on.
Demand/supply ratio
AVG_demand_index(zone, hour) / COUNT(available_drivers_in_zone) Demand index = trip requests in zone in rolling 30-min window
Ratio > 1.2: undersupplied — reposition drivers in. Ratio < 0.8: oversupplied — exit zone. Source: platform heatmap API or approximated from historical request patterns per zone-hour.

Operational Intelligence
The 5 critical mistakes, daily checklist, and +20% profit roadmap
Top 5 Mistakes Fleet Owners Make
1
Managing by revenue, not profit per vehicle.
VH-14 generates 2 150 kr/day yet loses 265 kr/day net. Revenue hides the loss. Track cost/km for every vehicle every single day without exception.
2
Ignoring idle time until it becomes a permanent habit.
A driver at 45% idle vs 25% target loses ~2 070 kr/month in potential output. Five drivers at that level = 10 350 kr/month in invisible lost revenue.
3
Single-platform dependency.
The 10% commission difference between Bolt and Uber compounds: at 187 trips/day that's 5 035 kr/day or 151 110 kr/month in margin left on the table by defaulting to Uber.
4
Reactive maintenance instead of scheduled.
Emergency repair = 3.1× the cost of scheduled service, plus 1–3 days lost revenue (~3 220 kr–9 660 kr). One breakdown event erases 3 months of saved maintenance budget.
5
No formal driver accountability system.
Bottom 20% of drivers generate 8% of revenue but consume 25% of operational support time. A scored ranking makes performance visible and consequences clear.
Daily Operational Checklist — 06:00
1
Confirm all scheduled drivers clocked in or absence notified. Activate on-call if >2 no-shows before 07:00.
2
Check vehicle downtime report. Workshop vehicles → reassign driver to standby vehicle immediately.
3
Review bottom 5 driver scores from yesterday. Repeat offenders (score <60 for 3+ days) → manager call today.
4
Check platform acceptance rate for all drivers. Any below 75% → contact before shift starts with zone and platform brief.
5
Pull demand forecast: events, weather, flights. Pre-position 3–4 drivers at airport before AM flight wave (06:30–08:30).
6
Mid-shift check 12:00: idle leaders and rev/hr laggards. Reposition or call within 30 min. No passive monitoring.
7
EOD 21:00: export P&L. Flag negative profit vehicles. Update driver leaderboard. Send personal stats digest to all drivers.
8
Confirm next-day maintenance slots booked. Notify impacted drivers of vehicle swaps with 12+ hours notice.
6 Levers to +20% Profit — With Numbers
+
Reduce idle time: 24% → 18%
Active repositioning for idle >10 min. +6–8% revenue zero new cost. At 55 430 kr/day: +3 325 kr–4 440 kr/day = +100 050 kr–133 400 kr/month.
+
Shift 15% more trips from Uber to Bolt
+27 kr net per shifted trip × 28 trips/day = +750 kr/day = +22 425 kr/month with zero behaviour change.
+
Replace top-2 loss vehicles (BMW/Audi → Skoda/Toyota class)
VH-14 loses 265 kr/day. Octavia equivalent would earn 2 095 kr/day. Delta: +2 360 kr/day or +70 725 kr/month per swap.
+
Airport pre-positioning 3× daily (AM/midday/PM)
Airport avg 415 kr vs 296 kr city. +6 airport trips/day = +706 kr net revenue = +21 170 kr/month.
+
Driver performance tiering with incentives
Top performers +2% bonus. Bottom 20% on formal improvement plans. Avg fleet score improvement 8–12 points. Reduces support overhead 3+ hrs/week.
+
Schedule 90%+ drivers for peak windows (07:30–09:30 and 17:00–20:00)
These 5 hours generate 34% more rev/hr than daily average. Full peak coverage potential: +4 600 kr–6 900 kr/day zero new cost.
